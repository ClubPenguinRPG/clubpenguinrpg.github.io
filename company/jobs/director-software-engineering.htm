Director, Software Engineering

The Director, Software Engineering is responsible for overseeing all software development of the shared platform of Disney Online Studios that drives the products Club Penguin, Pixie Hollow, Pirates of the Caribbean and Toontown. The teams include gaming technologies, business technology (e-commerce, affiliate, localization tools etc), customer support tools and community technologies (chat and friends list services).

 

Responsibilities:


Lead the central engineering teams for Disney Online Studios. Serve as a strong technical resource for central product development.

Plans and executes project work plans and revises as appropriate to meet changing needs and requirements. Manages day-to-day operational aspects of a project and scope working with project management.

Provide regular status reporting to project management and executive team. Build transparency within the teams for effective communication

Ensure development of procedures and policies for: architecture, configuration, and maintenance.

Hands-on management tasks, including hiring (identifying need, confirming the budget, submitting paperwork, shortlisting, interviewing and selecting), performance reviews and plans, task and project delegation, and staff support and monitoring.

 

Qualifications:

 

Bachelor’s or Master’s degree in Business Administration or Computer Science from an accredited university a firm requirement.

5+ years proven leadership in a Director or Management role specifically within software engineering in Java environment.

Experience in managing, building a team in multiple locations and driving projects.

Experience with high level architecture of web based software and n-tier applications.

Experience managing managers, developers, testers, and other types of engineers in a matrix organization.

Previous online gaming experience is a huge asset.

Experience working in a start-up environment an asset.

Technical knowledge of Java, Linux, MySQL, PHP and XML.

Experience in Agile.

Fluent in English.

 

Skills and Abilities:

 

Mix of management/director type skills as well as hands on architecture and strategy.

Expertise in evaluating and challenging technology decisions.

Able to speak into business objectives and translate them into technology related actionable items.

Experience and willingness to take calculated risks, while not afraid to admit when risks don’t pan out while quickly reversing course.

Strong leadership skills; able to manage a team located in different geographic locations.

Must be able to travel to other countries.

Able to delegate effectively.

 

Salary:

 

Competitive wage and benefits based on experience and qualifications.

 

Location:

 

Must be able to work in Kelowna, BC, Canada.

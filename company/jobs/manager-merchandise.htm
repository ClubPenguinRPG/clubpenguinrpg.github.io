Manager, Merchandise

This dynamic person will manage the merchandise department for Disney Online Studios (DOS) Canada, with regards to all Club Penguin (CP) products worldwide. This will include facilitating the day to day product approvals that are required, along with helping to oversee the merchandise department. This will entail liaising with the DOS Canada internal teams, as well as other Disney business units and their prospective licensees. The main objective will be to ensure that all aspects of the consumer product process are held to the highest standards and that all final products meet the quality and specifications that DOS Canada has envisioned for the Club Penguin consumer products line.

 

Responsibilities:

 

Partner with Merchandise, Brand, Creative, Club Penguin consumer product consultants, Disney Consumer Products, Disney Parks, Disney Stores, Disney Shopping (CP Online Shop) and Disney Interactive Studios and their prospective licensees.

Manages and facilitates the Club Penguin consumer products approvals process.

Maintain clear and transparent communication between internal DOS Canada departments and external partners. Be the project lead for all teams and executives for each consumer product.

Provide regular and accurate status on approvals and internal merchandise team to Director, Merchandise.

Ensure that requirements are clear and assets are provided to third party as agreed.

Identify and resolve conflict and risks with schedule, creative, resources, and design goals.

Develop and maintain professional and strong relationships within the industry and with all applicable vendors.

Keep on top of best practices within the industry.

Manage, coordinate, and facilitate multiple projects and process improvement initiatives related to the creation of Club Penguin consumer products.

 

Qualifications:

 

Relevant post-secondary education required.

3+ years related experience working as a merchandise manager in a consumer products company.

Experience working with licensees preferred.

Proficient with Microsoft Office tools (Outlook, Word, Excel, PowerPoint, etc) or equivalent in Mac platform.

Familiarity with Adobe CS Suite (Acrobat and Photoshop) would be a benefit.

Fluent in English.

 

Skills and Abilities:

 

Proven experience and understanding of consumer products industry and necessary approvals process including product life cycles.

Understands project management principles, practices, techniques, and tools.

Able to set objectives, prioritize and organize tasks and assign resources.

Excellent communication and interpersonal skills.

Strong negotiating skills and collaborative resolution skills.

Able to identify risks and apply appropriate risk management strategies.

Working knowledge of development of consumer products methodologies.

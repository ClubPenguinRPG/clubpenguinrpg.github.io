Administrative Assistant to Director, Finance

The Administrative Assistant to Director, Finance (AAF) will be supporting the Director of Finance and Accounting (DFA) and other members of the finance team by performing various administrative duties, which will involve organizing and maintaining the schedule of the DFA, dealing with confidential information, conducting research, creating reports, and maintaining information continuity.

 

Responsibilities:

 

Manage the calendar and daily tasks of the DFA, schedule meetings and appointments, and answer calls and emails.

Arrange travel and prepare expense report in SAP.

Assist accounting assistant in accounts payable and code Pos and Service entries in SAP.

Provide continuous support on financial related projects; assist the DFA on presentation material including updating confidential information on spreadsheets and other presentation software.

Develop and maintain a bring-forward and follow-up system for correspondence and ensure required action is initiated.

Conduct investigations and research, and prepare reports, minutes, and various correspondence.

 

Qualifications


Secondary diploma/degree or equivalent job experience and training are required.

3-5 years of progressive experience as an administrative assistant or related role.

Demonstrated technical competence with PC: Microsoft Word, Excel, and PowerPoint, Outlook and other office automation equipment.

Fluent in English.

 

Skills and Abilities:

 

Demonstrated high level administrative and executive support to senior management and ability to work effectively with all staff levels.

Excellent oral, written and interpersonal skills and the ability to communicate effectively and professionally to associates at all levels within the organization.

Strong organizational skills with the ability to prioritize and high level of attention to detail and follow-up.

Solid understanding of business professionalism and the ability to work in a team environment and an ability to handle diversity.

Ability to work effectively under pressure in a fast paced environment.

Willing to work flexible hours to meet challenging deadlines

Must be assertive and resourceful and have the discretion to handle confidential issues.

Proven problem solving ability with challenging deadlines. Ability to anticipate change and react efficiently and expeditiously.

 

Salary:

 

Competitive wage and benefits based on experience and qualifications

 

Location:

 

Must be able to work in Kelowna, BC, Canada

ActionScript Programmer, Art Department

The Art Department ActionScript Programmer oversees and collaborates with the development of new features, processes and workflows related to Flash programming tasks in the Art department.

 

Responsibilities:


Create interactive program elements for various in-game parties, rooms and supporting tools.

Consult on design, user experience, and software flow.

Mentor colleagues with Flash/programming and best practices within the department.

Communicate with Manager, Illustration & Animation and Team Leader, Interactive Experience & Set Design to ensure software is meeting expectations.

Mentor in Flash/programming within the department.

Other tasks as assigned by leaders.

 

Qualifications:

 

Bachelors degree in Computer Science or equivalent experience.

Strong Object Oriented Programming (OOP) background.

3+ years experience with Flash and programming in ActionScript 2.

Experience with:

  • Large scale application architecting.
  • Multi-language localization.
  • Version control and best practices.
  • Socket and asynchronous programming.
  • Bandwidth and friendly coding.

Automated testing/build/deployment experience an asset.

Proficient with Adobe Flash and issue tracking software.

Other programming languages such as PHP, JavaScript, Java, or JSFL an asset.

SmartFox knowledge an asset.

Fluent in English.

 

Skills and Abilities:

 

Exceptional Object Oriented Design (OOD) and software management principles.

Strong usability and design skills.

Works well under pressure to meet deadlines.

Strong attention to detail.

Accepts constructive criticism with openness to exploring new ideas and solutions.

Strong user experience design.

Generates creative solutions and tools.

Effective communication skills, both oral and written.

Writes accurate technical documents.

Strong team player.

 

Salary:

 

Competitive wage and benefits based on experience and qualifications

 

Location:

 

Must be able to work in Kelowna, BC, Canada

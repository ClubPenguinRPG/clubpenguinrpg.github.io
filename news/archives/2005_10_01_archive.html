Wednesday, October 26, 2005

Activation Help!

Hello Penguins,

We have had some people who are having trouble getting their activation codes. We have learned that Yahoo and Hotmail will almost always dump the activation email into the "Bulk" folder or the "Junk" folder. Please check those folders in your email program to find the activation code.

We have also learned that a lot of people enter email addresses that don't exist or that dont recieve email. Please make sure you enter a valid email address.

Also, you must activate your account (through the email) before you can get onto Club Penguin. This has changed from Penguin Chat. If you have tried everything and you still can get on, try this:

Try to login and when you get to the page with that says "Activation Required"
Please click on "lost your code" to get it resent.

If that still doesn't work, send an email to support@clubpenguin.com

Thanks for your patience with this.

Waddle on!

Friday, October 21, 2005

A little confusing...

When we added account authentication there was a feature added that allows parents of young children to restrict chatting to "easy-chat" only. We later realized that it automatically went to that method unless you changed it, so if you didn't read it and select the other method, you won't be able to chat.

So here's what to do if you meant to select "open-chat".

1. Log Into Club Penguin

2. Click on the "?" button in the menu bar


3. Click on the "edit account" button


4. Click on the "Safe Chat Level"


5. Please read and select the correct one. If a "parent password" box comes up, it means you entered a password into that box when you set up your account. You will need to enter the same password again to make the change.

Sorry for the confusion, hope this helps!

- Club Penguin Team

Tuesday, October 11, 2005

Good News and Bad News...

Whenever some one asks me that, I always want to hear the bad news first so here goes.

Over the last couple of weeks, we have been battling with how to handle this whole cloning problem. Some of you may be wondering "what cloning problem?" Well, when someone sets up multiple accounts its called cloning. Its one thing to have one or two, but many people have set up a lot more accounts. It is a problem for many reasons.

1- It uses up resources and slows down the whole chat. Because one person is taking up so much server power, it makes it worse for everyone
2- It makes it difficult to remove bad players. The agents and moderators have been doing a great job reporting when people are acting un-safe or using bad language. Unfortunately, we have watched a lot of people get kicked or banned and then just log in under a different account and keep breaking the rules. This wrecks it for everyone and makes us consider just using easy-chat only (which we tried for 12 hours last week).
3- It slows down development. When we are trying to manage accounts and someone has been cloning, it takes a lot more time, which means less time for making new games, etc.

Because of this slow-down, we probably wont hit our 2-week goal for launch and we didn't want to launch until this was dealt with.

Now for the good news, we have found a solution and rsnail and the team are working frantically (almost 20 hr days) to get it programmed and tested. It will require accounts to be authenticated using emails. Those of you who have ever played neo-pets will know what we are talking about. Basically it is the best way to solve this problem and it should be launched soon.

Thanks for your patience and we hope the wait hasn't been too painful. We will announce more details like the memberships soon. Until then, waddle-on!

Yours truly,

Club Penguin Team

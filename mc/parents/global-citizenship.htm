Kids Helping Kids

At Club Penguin, we’re committed to improving the lives of children everywhere by supporting organizations doing important work on behalf of the world’s youngest and most vulnerable citizens.

A portion of the proceeds from every Club Penguin membership is used to support specific local and global projects. Currently, our funds are focused on two building projects in a small Ugandan village and a children’s hospital project in Romania.

Supporting these projects is our way of allowing the kids who join Club Penguin to play a role in securing a happier and healthier future for other children around the globe. It’s kids helping kids, something we can all be proud of.

We are currently organizing a non-profit foundation to manage the specifics of each project we undertake. This will allow us to provide you with more detailed, timely updates on projects and the difference these projects are making in many kids lives. Check back for updates.

Uganda

Club Penguin’s newest Kids Helping Kids project will improve the lives of African children orphaned by AIDS and war.

Education

We are working with an organization in Uganda to help provide scholarships, project funding and learning resources to improve education opportunities for students across the country. We are planning a pilot project, “Kids Helping Kids Resource Center” to address the need for children to have FREE access to quality educational and academic resources, including but not limited to: computers, internet, age-appropriate reading materials, and a safe place to explore.

Health Care

We have committed to build a medical facility in Fort Portal that will provide children better access to basic medical treatment. We have also helped equip a medical clinic in Rwebisengo, a village along the Ugandan border with Congo.

Romania

Home Makeover

In Fall 2006, Club Penguin partnered with an international organization and Romanian social workers to improve the home of one family in the village of Budila. The renovation has provided this family with a healthier and more stable environment.

Brasov Children’s Hospital

Overcrowded hospitals and abandoned children, combined with a severe shortage of orphanages and foster care, has left a great number of Romanian children in need of help.

Club Penguin is helping one organization address this need through the Brasov Children’s Hospital Project. This undertaking will provide care for abandoned children and work toward reintegrating children with their families where possible.

Renovation of hospital rooms has begun and a team of social workers and caregivers is in place to help.

Meal and Education Program

This project provides children who are having difficulty in school and combating malnutrition with extra help in several villages and towns.

Children are selected by the local school board, and given the opportunity to attend the program where they receive lunch, after class help, computer training and specialized one on one care.

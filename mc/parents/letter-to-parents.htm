Safety Letter to Parents


Dear Parents,

With Internet use among young people continuing to grow at a rapid pace, concerns about online safety for children are at an all time high. As parents of young children ourselves, we at Club Penguin understand and share these concerns. This is exactly what motivated us to create a fun, online world we’d be comfortable letting our own kids visit.

At Club Penguin, we’re dedicated to safeguarding any personal information collected online and we’ve taken steps to ensure participants can’t post their contact information. Thanks to those measures, and to our sophisticated filtering system, live moderators and strict privacy policy, we’re confident Club Penguin offers one of the safest online environments to date.

As a testament to our efforts, Club Penguin was chosen by the Better Business Bureau’s BBB OnLine program to receive its Kid’s Privacy Seal of Approval, a coveted designation currently held by less than a dozen other companies in the world. We’re also proud to partner with NetSmartz ®, an interactive, educational safety resource from the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children ® and Boys & Girls Clubs of America for children (ages 5-17), parents, guardians, educators, and law enforcement that uses age-appropriate, 3-D activities to teach Internet safety.

While the Internet has opened up an exciting new world of educational, entertainment and social opportunities, online activity does not come without risk. Our goal is to minimize that risk as much as possible and protect Club Penguin users by continually upgrading our systems and policies.

We recognize, however, that no system or policy is foolproof. It is our belief that there can be no substitute for parental involvement, and we strongly encourage parents to educate themselves and their children on responsible use of the Internet and to monitor all their children’s online activities.

Please feel free to email us at if you require any further information.

Sincerely,

The Club Penguin Team

About Club Penguin

In March 2005, the founders of Club Penguin set out to create an ad-free, virtual world where children could play games, have fun and interact. As Internet specialists and parents, they wanted Club Penguin to be a place they’d feel comfortable letting their own children and grandchildren visit.

After months of consultation, research and testing, Club Penguin opened to the public in October 2005. Thanks to great reviews from players and parents, word quickly spread. Club Penguin launched on Miniclip, the world’s largest online game site in March 2006, and rapidly became Miniclip’s number one game.

In August 2007, Club Penguin joined the Walt Disney Company in a partnership designed to provide access to unprecedented resources and unique development opportunities.

Club Penguin is privileged to be part of the Walt Disney Company, which shares its unwavering commitment to creating a fun and safe online entertainment experience for children and families.

Club Penguin is passionate about community engagement works hard to foster a culture of giving, both in the virtual world of Club Penguin and within the company.

Club Penguin is headquartered in the beautiful city of Kelowna, British Columbia, Canada and also maintains an office and staff in Brighton, England.

Corporate Biographies

 

Dave Krysko, Senior Vice President, Walt Disney Internet Group, Co-Founder and Senior Vice President, Business Operations, Club Penguin

Dave Krysko is senior vice president of business operations and one of three co-founders of Club Penguin, one of the most popular and fastest-growing virtual worlds for children.

Dave helped develop and launch Club Penguin in 2005 and played an integral role in its phenomenal growth and success. The company’s commitment to creating a safe and ad-free online playground helped propel Club Penguin to international acclaim and a partnership with the Walt Disney Company, which acquired Club Penguin in August 2007.

Prior to his work with Club Penguin and the Walt Disney Internet Group, Dave was a professional musician and worked in the audio and video production business. It was during the time he owned and operated New Horizon Productions in Kelowna, British Columbia, Canada that Dave partnered with his then employees Lane Merrifield and Lance Priebe to develop Club Penguin.

Dave is a longtime philanthropist who has been involved in humanitarian work in Canada, Mexico and Romania. In addition to helping develop the strategic direction of Club Penguin, he devotes a substantial amount of time and energy into managing the New Horizon Foundation, a charitable organization created and funded by Club Penguin and committed to improving the lives of children and their families around the world.

 

Lane Merrifield, Executive Vice President (Walt Disney Internet Group) and General Manager (Club Penguin)

Lane Merrifield is is one of three co-founders of Club Penguin, and, prior to the company’s acquisition by the Walt Disney Company, served as its chief executive officer.

Born in Lethbridge, Alberta, Canada, Lane moved to Southern California as a child and was bitten by the technology bug very early in life. Beginning with his first job at Disneyland, Lane took on various technical and marketing roles with a range of companies. In 2003, he and his family moved back to Canada to settle in Kelowna and work for New Horizon Productions (the predecessor to New Horizon Interactive, which created Club Penguin).

Lane has always nurtured a passion for helping people understand and use technology. He believes Club Penguin has the potential to bring communities together and loves that at any given moment, children around the world are experiencing so much enjoyment with something he helped build.

 

Lance Priebe, Senior Vice President (Walt Disney Internet Group) and Chief Creative Officer (Club Penguin)

Lance Priebe is one of three co-founders of Club Penguin, and prior to the company’s acquisition by the Walt Disney Company, served as its chief technical officer.

Born in Kitimat, British Columbia, Canada, Lance moved to Kelowna, British Columbia in 1994 and began his career as a graphic artist, working in large format, digital imaging and printing before forming his own graphic art and web design company. In 1999, Lance went to work at New Horizon Productions (the predecessor to New Horizon Interactive, which created Club Penguin.

Lance is the creative mind behind the original concept and design of Club Penguin, In his early days at New Horizon, Lance spent his time off work experimenting with the concept of Flash-based web games that required no downloads to play. One of his first products, a basic chat and interaction game called “Experimental Penguins”, became the template upon which Club Penguin was eventually built.
